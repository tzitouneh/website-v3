Hobbies

Gaming

I've been playing video games for as long as I can remember. I’ve learned communication, problem solving, and time management skills. My favorite game has to be Trials Fusion. It has a steep learning curve, but once you understand the physics and how your bike interacts with the various platforms, the game becomes very rewarding. It can feel random at times, but once you understand the techniques being used then it's just a matter of practice and the results are repeatable. The game has a lot of challenges and it seems impossible at first, but watching the leaderboards and other people’s attempts motivates you because it shows that it can be done.

Tech

My first console was the Game Boy Advance. This was the beginning of my fascination with technology. From then on I’ve accumulated more consoles like the Nintendo DS, Wii, PSP, PS3, PS4, and eventually I decided I would build my own computer to play video games. This opened up a whole new door. I learned about the various components that are needed and how they all communicate with each other. Seeing how you could customize your computer to your needs juxtaposed the uniformity of consoles. Building my first computer was such a memorable experience, and I recommend everyone doing it at least once.

Research

I enjoy studying history and theology, and when I can, write research papers on topics I'm passionate about. Recently, I’ve been spreading awareness about the genocide happening in ChinaI where three million Uyghurs are in concentration camps. They are forced to denounce their religion, and are not allowed to practice their religion how they are meant to be. Women are being sterilized and raped. Families are separated. And there's mass torture. I’ve been trying to set up an event on campus on this and how we can help.
